## Projects

### ESG Risks

#### Overview:
This project identifies financial and environmental risks within corporate datasets. Using Python, I built a data pipeline that processes company revenue, profit, and carbon emission data to flag "High Risk" anomalies—companies that are financially or environmentally inefficient compared to their peers.
#### Goal:
Automate the "Digital Finance Transformation" process by moving from manual spreadsheet checks to an automated Machine Learning model.

#### Findings:
During 2015–2025, Energy and Utilities remain the highest carbon intensity sectors, the narrowing of the box plot "body" over this time period indicates a shift toward industry-wide standardization in emissions reporting and carbon efficiency. In 2025, despite having 79 "Efficient" entities, the Energy sector contains the highest volume of High Risk companies (29), signaling an urgent need for targeted green investment.  The Technology sector maintains a low median intensity throughout the period, however the model flagged 12 High Risk entities. These represent outliers—likely data centers or hardware manufacturers—whose energy consumption is disproportionately high relative to their profit margins. Healthcare and Manufacturing have successfully integrated ESG-driven controls, showing nearly 100% efficiency ratings by 2025.

#### Tools: 
Python, Numpy, Pandas, Scikit-Learn, Plotly
